#### Interoperability test

**T1.** Determine whether C11 accurately characterizes Gelenbe's CPN and whether the proposed architecture truly reverses it, after testing the claim against earlier systems whose transported unit carried durable purpose, authority, resumable state, and a way home independently of its current processor.

#### Finding

**Disposition recommended: narrow C11, do not withdraw it.** Its account of CPN is substantially fair if "payload" means the user or application payload, and the proposed architecture is a genuine *relative* inversion of CPN. It is not a clean historical inversion of all prior distributed systems, because 1990s mobile-agent systems already moved a close combination of purposeful code, execution state, objects, and delegated authority.

**(a) Fairness to Gelenbe.** CPN separates exploratory smart packets, acknowledgements, and payload-carrying dumb packets. Smart packets pursue QoS objectives and gather routing measurements; acknowledgements return discovered route information; dumb packets carry the application data along learned routes. Thus CPN's cognitive mechanism is directed at transport control rather than at performing the application-level inquiry represented by this paper's packet. Two qualifications are needed. First, the smart packet is not "ordinary data": it already carries a goal concerning its journey and accumulates state. Second, "the packet thinks" is metaphorical; learning is jointly realized by packets and node-level random-neural-network mailboxes. The sentence "the payload remains data" is fair only when explicitly restricted to *application payload*.

**(b) Prior art and the inversion.** The strongest near-counterexample found is **Telescript**, considered with the broader mobile-agent literature. Thorn's survey describes an agent being transmitted with its objects and resuming execution at a destination only if that destination accepts its authority and permits. Contemporary descriptions of Telescript's `go` operation likewise treat migration as suspension, packaging of internal execution state, transfer, and resumption. The wider mobile-agent literature explicitly describes agents as carrying code, data/state, and authority to act for an owner. CNRI's Knowbot Programs are another close relative: self-contained mobile agents, potentially written in multiple languages, interoperating at service stations under defined rules.

This prior art satisfies much of the packet's bar:

- **purpose:** a task was embodied in the agent's program, though usually procedurally rather than as a declarative, inspectable goal field;
- **mandate:** Telescript authority and destination permits formed an explicit permission structure;
- **resumable state:** strong migration captured enough execution state to resume elsewhere;
- **return:** agents could carry itineraries, report results, or program a return to their origin, but a durable home and mandatory return path were not generally architectural invariants;
- **processor replacement:** this is the decisive shortfall. The mobile agent normally *was* the moving program/process. It resumed inside a compatible execution environment; an independently implemented human, model, or tool did not substitute for it by reading a processor-neutral work object.

Accordingly, no exact counterexample was found that meets all four requested properties as first-class content **and** preserves the work across replacement of the current processor. But mobile agents are too close for §3's unqualified "None of them made the travelling work itself the persistent unit" to remain credible without discussion. The defensible novelty is not that purpose, authority, state, or return had never travelled. It is their consolidation in a durable *work object separated from its processor*, with explicit completion, provenance, and return-to-home semantics across heterogeneous processors.

#### Proposed revision of C11

> **C11 (Historical/architectural claim).** Gelenbe's Cognitive Packet Networks placed adaptive cognition in transport control: smart packets explored routes under QoS goals and acknowledgements returned route information, while dumb packets carried application data. This paper performs a relative inversion by making purposeful cognitive work the durable application-level payload. Unlike classical mobile-agent systems, which moved a program with its execution state and delegated authority inside a compatible runtime, the proposed packet keeps the work object distinct from any current processor and makes its goal, mandate, completion conditions, provenance, and return-to-home explicit.

The broad sentence in §3 should also be narrowed, for example:

> Mobile-agent systems came close: they moved purposeful programs together with state and, in systems such as Telescript, explicit authority. What they ordinarily made persistent was the mobile program or process itself. The present proposal instead makes the work object persistent and processor-independent, so that heterogeneous humans, models, tools, or institutions may substitute for one another without owning its continuity.
