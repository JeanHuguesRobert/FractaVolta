## Abstract

The Generalized Packet Networks framework provides a cross-domain vocabulary for analyzing bounded, addressable, storable, routable, and resource-occupying units of operational usefulness. However, a general framework becomes useful only when it can be rigorously declined into substrate-specific papers: thermal packets, electric packets, hydraulic packets, inference packets, cognitive packets, mandate packets, monetary packets, and other candidates.

This document provides a minimal template for producing such substrate-specific packet papers. It is not an academic article in itself. It is a methodological tool. Its purpose is to prevent metaphorical overreach, enforce operational boundaries, require service metrics, expose failure modes, and make each new packet paper compatible with the FractaVolta / Fractanet / Cogentia Commons corpus.

The template is designed under the *Seconde méthode*: claims are versioned, objections are first-class contributions, and every paper must remain open to adversarial review. A candidate packet domain should not be promoted to a full paper until it passes the operational boundary rule and can fill the packet service metric template with at least partially measurable quantities.

## 1. Purpose

This template turns the Generalized Packet Networks framework into a repeatable production method.

The goal is not to generate more papers by analogy. The goal is to generate **better disciplined** papers by forcing each candidate domain to answer the same operational questions:

```text
What is the packet?
Why is it a packet rather than a continuous flow, relation, or metaphor?
What does it contain?
What resource does it occupy?
How is it routed?
How does it degrade?
How is it measured?
Where does it fail?
What does this domain add back to the general framework?
```

The template therefore serves four functions:

1. **Boundary discipline** — prevent “everything is a packet” inflation.
2. **Metric discipline** — require measurable or at least operational quantities.
3. **Corpus discipline** — connect each paper to the wider FractaVolta / Fractanet / Cogentia corpus.
4. **Method discipline** — make improvement itself reusable, versioned, and transmissible.

The template is a meta-document: it does not only produce content; it produces a method for producing content.

## 2. When to Use This Template

Use this template when proposing a substrate-specific paper derived from Generalized Packet Networks.

Examples:

| Candidate paper | Minimal packet |
|---|---|
| Thermal Packet Networks | insulated bottle, hot-water tank |
| Electric Packet Networks | battery, powerbank, electric vehicle |
| Packetized Gravity Networks | water mass at altitude |
| Hydric Packet Networks | potable-water bottle, tanker |
| Inference Packet Networks | AI request, executable context |
| Cognitive Packets | continuation payload |
| Material / Logistics Packet Networks | parcel, pallet, container |
| Cold-Chain Packet Networks | refrigerated item, vaccine, ice module |
| Monetary / Value Packet Networks | payment, token, donation |
| Mandate Packet Networks | vote, delegation, revocable mandate |
| Legal / Administrative Packet Networks | complaint, claim, authorization, decision |
| Emergency / Resilience Packet Networks | kit, alert, water, battery, medical supply |

Do not use the template to force a domain into packet language merely because it is interesting.

A candidate domain should remain exploratory if:

- the packet cannot be bounded;
- the packet cannot be addressed;
- the packet cannot be delayed, cached, buffered, rerouted, retired, or audited independently;
- the relevant resource occupancy cannot be identified;
- no failure mode can be made explicit;
- the domain collapses into metaphor before becoming operational.

## 3. Minimal Packet Example

Every substrate-specific paper should begin with a **minimal packet example**.

The minimal packet is the smallest, most concrete, least speculative instance of the proposed packet type.

Examples:

| Domain | Good minimal packet | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| Thermal | insulated bottle | bounded heat/cold, decay, portability |
| Electric | powerbank | bounded electrical storage, charge/discharge |
| Hydric | bottle of potable water | bounded water, sanitary status, delivery |
| Gravity | water mass stored at altitude | bounded potential energy |
| Inference | single AI request | bounded task occupying compute |
| Cognitive | continuation payload | bounded context enabling task resumption |
| Monetary | payment instruction | bounded value transfer |
| Governance | signed time-bounded mandate | bounded authority with validity |
| Legal | filed complaint | bounded claim entering procedure |

A strong minimal packet prevents abstraction drift. It gives the paper an anchor that a skeptical reader can inspect.

### 3.1 Minimal Packet Test

A minimal packet should answer:

```text
What is inside?
What contains it?
Who or what can identify it?
Can it be stored?
Can it be delayed?
Can it be routed or assigned?
Can it be used, consumed, discharged, validated, or executed?
Can it be lost, degraded, expired, revoked, or retired?
```

If the answer to most of these questions is unclear, the candidate is not ready for a substrate-specific paper.

## 4. Operational Boundary Rule

A unit qualifies as a **packet** rather than a continuous flow, vague relation, or metaphor only when its primary operational identity is carried by bounded, addressable, storable, and independently routable discreteness.

### 4.1 Primary Rule

A candidate packet must be capable of being:

- bounded;
- identified;
- stored;
- delayed;
- buffered or cached;
- routed or assigned;
- measured or audited;
- transformed, consumed, discharged, executed, validated, or retired.

### 4.2 Primary Test

The candidate qualifies as a packet if it can be delayed, cached, buffered, rerouted, transformed, audited, revoked, or retired independently of the substrate flow without losing its operational usefulness.

### 4.3 Examples

| Candidate | Packet? | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| IP packet | yes | bounded, addressed, routed, dropped, delayed |
| Analog electrical signal | usually no | primarily continuous, not independently packetized |
| Powerbank | yes | bounded energy, charge/discharge, portable |
| Continuous current in a wire | no, unless discretized | flow rather than packet |
| Insulated bottle | yes | bounded heat/cold, stored, transported |
| Hot water flowing in a pipe | usually no | primarily continuous circuit flow |
| Payment instruction | yes | bounded value transfer, auditable |
| Vague debt relationship | not yet | needs discretization into claim, invoice, token, settlement |
| Signed mandate | yes | bounded authority, scope, validity |
| Diffuse influence | no | not bounded unless expressed through identifiable acts |
| Social media post | yes | bounded content, propagation, decay |
| General public mood | no | emergent condition, not a packet |

This rule is the main defense against conceptual inflation.

## 5. Substrate-Specific Non-Claims

Every packet paper must state what it does **not** claim.

This prevents the general framework from erasing the substrate.

Examples:

### 5.1 Thermal Paper

A thermal packet paper should not claim that heat behaves like data. Heat dissipates, has temperature thresholds, requires insulation, and is constrained by mass, volume, and exergy.

### 5.2 Electric Paper

An electric packet paper should not claim that batteries are equivalent to IP packets. Batteries have chemistry, degradation, charge-rate limits, safety constraints, and lifecycle costs.

### 5.3 Inference Paper

An inference packet paper should not claim that AI requests are just data packets. They occupy model state, context windows, memory bandwidth, energy, latency budgets, and may have semantic validity constraints.

### 5.4 Governance Paper

A mandate packet paper should not claim that authority is merely information. Mandates require legitimacy, procedure, accountability, revocability, and institutional recognition.

### 5.5 General Formula

```text
This paper uses packet vocabulary only where it reveals operational structure.
It does not claim physical, institutional, or ethical identity with other packet domains.
```

A substrate-specific paper should always include at least five non-claims.

## 6. Packet Service Metric Template

Every substrate-specific packet paper should fill the same minimal metric template.

A paper that cannot fill this table should remain exploratory. A paper that fills it with measured or measurable quantities becomes operational.

| Metric | Question | Generic form | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Packet capacity | What does the packet contain? | quantity per packet | bytes, kWh, kg, tokens, votes |
| Addressability | How is it identified or routed? | address / identifier / scope | IP address, wallet, mandate ID |
| Transfer interface | How does it enter or leave the system? | connector / protocol / procedure | port, API, signature, docking |
| Transfer rate | How fast can it be moved or processed? | packet quantity / time | kW, packets/s, parcels/h |
| Resource occupancy | What does it occupy, and for how long? | A = λ · h | port time, GPU time, road lane, attention |
| Congestion elasticity | How fast does performance degrade near saturation? | (dC/C)/(dA/A) | delay, loss, queue, cost |
| Decay / freshness | How does usefulness decline over time? | v(t), TTL, expiry | heat loss, news staleness, mandate expiry |
| Hot/cold gradient | How close or fast must it remain? | access latency / priority | cache, local stock, archive |
| Buffer role | Does it absorb mismatch? | inflow/outflow smoothing | tank, queue, backlog |
| Cache role | Does it keep usefulness near demand? | proximity / expected reuse | local stock, context cache |
| Loss mode | How is it lost or degraded? | failure mode | leakage, drop, spoilage, invalidity |
| Governance | Who is responsible? | attribution / audit / revocation | owner, issuer, delegate, operator |
| Lifecycle cost | What does it cost to create, move, store, retire? | cost per packet or service unit | €/kWh, €/request, €/parcel |
| Externalities | What harms or benefits does it produce? | emissions, risk, capture | CO₂, congestion, opacity |

### 6.1 Minimal Mathematical Layer

Where possible, the paper should include simple equations.

Examples:

#### Resource occupancy

```text
A = λ · h
```

where `λ` is packet arrival rate and `h` is mean holding time.

#### Congestion elasticity

```text
ε = (dC / C) / (dA / A)
```

where `C` is congestion and `A` is traffic intensity.

#### Packet decay

```text
v(t) = v0 · e^(-t/τ)
```

or a more appropriate substrate-specific decay function.

#### Transfer time

```text
t = quantity / transfer_rate
```

#### Service cost

```text
cost_per_service_unit = cycle_cost / useful_quantity_delivered
```

The goal is not to over-mathematize. The goal is to expose the operational levers.

## 7. Worked Example Requirement

Every substrate-specific paper should contain at least one worked example.

A worked example should be:

- concrete;
- small enough to verify;
- preferably numerical;
- linked to the service metric template;
- honest about limits.

Examples:

| Domain | Possible worked example |
|---|---|
| Thermal | 1 L thermos cooling over 6 h |
| Electric | 20,000 mAh powerbank charging a phone |
| Hydric | 1,000 L potable-water tank delivery |
| Gravity | 1 m³ water lifted 100 m |
| Inference | one model request occupying GPU for 2 s |
| Cognitive | one continuation moving a task between agents |
| Monetary | one Kudos donation linked to an euro transaction |
| Mandate | one revocable delegation with expiry |
| Legal | one complaint entering an administrative queue |

### 7.1 Worked Example Checklist

```text
Initial quantity
Container or carrier
Interface
Transfer or processing time
Resource occupied
Decay or validity horizon
Failure modes
Measurement
Governance / responsibility
```

A worked example is not an illustration. It is a stress test.

## 8. Failure Modes

Every substrate-specific paper should include a ranked list of failure modes.

Failure modes protect the paper against rhetorical overreach.

### 8.1 Generic Failure Modes

| Failure mode | Description |
|---|---|
| Boundary failure | candidate is not actually a packet |
| Metric failure | no useful quantities can be measured |
| Substrate erasure | analogy ignores physical or institutional constraints |
| Density failure | packet too diffuse to transport or store efficiently |
| Transfer failure | interface too slow, costly, dangerous, or complex |
| Decay failure | packet loses usefulness before delivery |
| Congestion failure | system saturates too quickly |
| Governance failure | responsibility, validity, or revocation unclear |
| Security failure | packet can be spoofed, captured, corrupted, or weaponized |
| Lifecycle failure | costs or externalities exceed benefits |
| Scope creep | paper becomes a theory of everything |

### 8.2 Ranking Requirement

Failure modes should be ranked:

```text
critical / major / minor
```

or:

```text
high probability / medium probability / low probability
```

For example, in Thermal Packet Networks, low volumetric energy density is critical. In Mandate Packet Networks, legitimacy failure is critical. In Inference Packet Networks, compute bottlenecks and semantic staleness may be critical.

## 9. Corpus Links

A packet paper must declare its relation to the corpus.

### 9.1 What It Receives

A paper should say what it receives from the existing corpus:

- vocabulary from Generalized Packet Networks;
- energy framing from FractaVolta;
- cognitive method from Cogentia;
- exergy framing from Mare Nostrum;
- governance framing from Kudocracy;
- value framing from Kudos;
- continuation method from Cognitive Packets;
- critique method from the Seconde méthode.

### 9.2 What It Gives Back

A paper should also say what it contributes back.

Examples:

| Paper | Gives back to GPN |
|---|---|
| Thermal Packet Networks | physical decay, exergy match, temperature thresholds |
| Electric Packet Networks | charge rates, battery degradation, grid interaction |
| Inference Packet Networks | compute occupancy, latency, context staleness |
| Mandate Packet Networks | legitimacy, revocation, authority traceability |
| Hydric Packet Networks | sanitary status, vital resource priority |
| Cognitive Packets | resumability, provenance, continuation payloads |

### 9.3 Bidirectional Link Paragraph

Each paper should include a paragraph of the form:

```markdown
This paper receives its packet vocabulary from Generalized Packet Networks. Conversely, it contributes back to GPN by forcing the framework to account for [substrate-specific constraints]. It therefore functions both as an instance of the general framework and as a stress test of that framework.
```

## 10. Claim Manifest

Every packet paper should include a versioned claim manifest.

A claim manifest distinguishes:

- observation;
- definition;
- operational rule;
- metric;
- hypothesis;
- non-claim;
- research programme;
- implementation claim.

### 10.1 Template

```markdown
## Claim Manifest — vX.Y

| Claim | Status | Description |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Observation | ... |
| C2 | Definition | ... |
| C3 | Operational rule | ... |
| C4 | Metric | ... |
| C5 | Hypothesis | ... |
| C6 | Non-claim | ... |
| C7 | Research programme | ... |
```

### 10.2 Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Observation | recurring pattern seen across examples |
| Definition | terminology introduced by the paper |
| Operational rule | boundary or test used to decide applicability |
| Metric | measurable or computable quantity |
| Hypothesis | plausible but not yet demonstrated claim |
| Non-claim | explicit limit of the paper |
| Research programme | future work to test or develop |
| Implementation claim | claim tied to a project, tool, pilot, or infrastructure |

### 10.3 Example Claim Manifest

```markdown
## Claim Manifest — v0.1

| Claim | Status | Description |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Definition | A [substrate] packet is defined as ... |
| C2 | Operational rule | It qualifies as a packet only if ... |
| C3 | Observation | The same grammar appears at multiple scales: ... |
| C4 | Metric | The paper introduces [metric] to measure ... |
| C5 | Hypothesis | The packet architecture may outperform circuits when ... |
| C6 | Non-claim | The paper does not claim ... |
| C7 | Research programme | Future work should test ... |
```

The claim manifest is a compact interface for critique.

## 11. Seconde Méthode Review Prompt

Every packet paper should be submitted to adversarial review using a stable prompt.

### 11.1 Review Prompt

```text
Please critically evaluate the following working paper as if you were a skeptical but constructive academic reviewer.

Title: [TITLE]

Context:
This paper applies the Generalized Packet Networks framework to [SUBSTRATE]. It defines a [SUBSTRATE] packet as [DEFINITION]. It claims that this substrate can be analyzed through bounded packets, resource occupancy, service metrics, congestion, decay/freshness, topology, and governance constraints. The paper explicitly does not claim that [SUBSTRATE] behaves like information packets or that packetization is always desirable.

Please assess:

1. Originality relative to the closest existing literature.
2. Whether the operational boundary rule is crisp enough.
3. Whether the minimal packet example is well chosen.
4. Scientific or technical rigor.
5. Risk of metaphorical overreach.
6. Weakest technical assumptions.
7. Missing literature.
8. Whether the service metric template is properly filled.
9. Whether the failure modes are honest and complete.
10. Whether the corpus links are useful or distracting.
11. Whether the claim manifest is clear.
12. What should be removed, clarified, or strengthened before public release.
13. A “bullshit meter” from 0 to 10, where 0 means rigorous and 10 means mostly empty rhetoric.

Please be direct and critical.
```

### 11.2 Output Expected From Review

The review should produce:

- strongest objections;
- conceptual overextensions;
- missing references;
- concrete v-next edits;
- method improvements;
- bullshit meter;
- recommendation: abandon / revise / publish as working paper / submit externally.

## 13. Candidate Selection Rule

A future packet paper should be attempted only if the candidate domain passes three filters.

### 13.1 Boundary Filter

Can the candidate be bounded, addressed, stored, delayed, routed, audited, retired, or revoked independently?

### 13.2 Metric Filter

Can the service metric template be filled with meaningful values?

### 13.3 Failure Filter

Can one identify cases where the packet framing fails?

If the answer to any of these is no, the candidate should remain exploratory.

## 14. Self-Application of This Template

This template is itself a packet. More precisely, it is a **method packet**: a bounded, addressable, reusable unit of procedural knowledge designed to generate substrate-specific packet papers.

It therefore applies a lightweight version of its own rules to itself.

### 14.1 Minimal packet

The minimal packet is the file itself:

```text
packet_paper_template.md
```

It is bounded, versioned, stored in a repository, addressable through a canonical URL, transmissible to humans or agents, and executable as a writing method.

### 14.2 Packet type

The template can be classified as:

```text
method packet
→ cognitive packet
→ generative corpus packet
```

It contains not only information, but a procedure: how to produce other disciplined documents.

### 14.3 Boundary rule

The template qualifies as a packet because it can be:

- bounded as a Markdown file;
- addressed through a file path and canonical URL;
- versioned through Git;
- stored, copied, forked, reviewed, and archived;
- routed to humans, artificial agents, or repositories;
- executed cognitively as a writing procedure;
- improved through issues, pull requests, comments, and continuations;
- retired or replaced by a later version.

### 14.4 Non-claims

This template does not claim to guarantee scientific validity.

It does not replace substrate-specific expertise.

It does not automatically prevent metaphorical overreach.

It does not prove that a candidate packet domain is legitimate.

It does not remove the need for literature review, worked examples, failure modes, or adversarial critique.

It provides a disciplined procedure, not a certificate of truth.

### 14.5 Failure modes

The template may fail if it:

- becomes bureaucratic rather than clarifying;
- produces mechanically uniform papers;
- gives an illusion of rigor without actual measurements;
- encourages excessive proliferation of substrate papers;
- hides substrate-specific constraints under a common vocabulary;
- is applied without adversarial review;
- is treated as a finished method rather than an evolving artifact.

### 14.6 Self claim manifest

| Claim | Status | Description |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Definition | This template is a method packet. |
| C2 | Operational rule | It passes the packet boundary rule as a bounded, addressable, versioned, reusable procedural unit. |
| C3 | Method claim | It provides a minimal procedure for producing substrate-specific packet papers. |
| C4 | Non-claim | It does not guarantee scientific validity or replace domain expertise. |
| C5 | Failure mode | It can become bureaucratic, inflationary, or falsely reassuring if applied mechanically. |
| C6 | Research programme | It should be revised after each serious substrate-paper iteration. |

A method serious enough to constrain other documents must tolerate being applied to itself. It should not, however, become trapped in self-reference. Self-application is a calibration step, not the main purpose of the template.

## 15. Relation to Cogentia Commons

This template is itself a cognitive packet.

It contains enough context, structure, procedure, constraints, and continuation instructions to allow another human or artificial agent to produce a substrate-specific paper without restarting from zero.

In Cogentia Commons terms, the template functions as:

- a continuation scaffold;
- a method packet;
- a corpus interface;
- a critique protocol;
- a reproducible writing procedure.

This is why the method matters. The goal is not merely to write more documents. The goal is to create a corpus that can be resumed, criticized, improved, and extended coherently across conversations, agents, repositories, and time.

A concise formulation:

> The method becomes an artifact of the corpus.

## 18. Final Formula

The Generalized Packet Networks paper defines the grammar.

Substrate-specific packet papers write sentences in that grammar.

This template teaches how to write those sentences without losing rigor.

```text
GPN = theory
TPN / EPN / IPN / MPN = instances
Packet Paper Template = method of instantiation
Cogentia Commons = infrastructure for continuation and improvement
```

The template is not ancillary. It is the mechanism by which the corpus becomes generative without becoming incoherent.

> A theory becomes productive when it can generate disciplined instances.
