# FractaVolta – Partner  Brief  
## Experimental Micro-Agrivoltaics Pilot Opportunity

FractaVolta invites collaboration on a controlled pilot to test a novel intra-row agrivoltaic configuration designed for mountain viticulture environments.

This experimental system alternates vertical bifacial PV modules and vine plants within the same trellised row, operating at plant-scale spatial resolution. The pilot will validate physical feasibility, measure local interactions, and assess operational compatibility under real field conditions.

We seek technical partners to co-develop and instrument this reversible demonstration, with clear pathways toward broader applications in energy, agriculture, digital infrastructure, and territorial regeneration.

***

## Pilot Overview

### The core proposition
The system integrates standard vineyard trellis rows with vertical bifacial photovoltaic modules aligned along the same axis. Vines and PV elements alternate at controlled plant-scale intervals, creating a linear hybrid structure without overhead shading or continuous PV fencing.

This approach differs fundamentally from conventional agrivoltaics. It avoids parcel-scale homogeneity and focuses instead on discrete, local interactions between biological and technical components.

### Physical configuration
**Photovoltaic subsystem**: Vertical bifacial modules capture morning/evening irradiance while maintaining airflow transparency and minimal ground footprint.

**Viticulture subsystem**: Standard trellis with preserved agricultural operations serving as baseline control.

**Integration**: Co-linear placement within existing rows. No canopy modification. Fully reversible installation.

### Expected interactions to measure
The pilot targets coupled effects at micro-scale:
- Localized shading patterns and modified irradiance along the row.
- Wind modulation, temperature gradients, and humidity redistribution near PV structures.
- Heterogeneous vine response in photosynthesis, vigor, and phenological development.

### Instrumentation framework
**Energy**: Real-time kWh production, diurnal curves, availability indicators.  
**Environment**: Irradiance, row-level temperature/humidity, wind estimation.  
**Agronomy**: Vine vigor, phenology, yield variability by row section.

**Constraints**: Single row or small cluster footprint. No permanent soil modification. Mechanical operation compatibility.

***

## Technical Positioning

Current agrivoltaic systems emphasize overhead structures and parcel-scale shading control. Vertical PV applications target open-field crops or pasture systems.

FractaVolta addresses an unexplored gap: plant-scale alternation of vines and vertical PV within the same row structure. No established framework currently exists for this configuration in mountain viticulture contexts.

## Pilot Objectives (Specific and Measurable)

1. **Physical feasibility**: Confirm stable co-existence of vine/PV alternation under mountain conditions.
2. **Interaction characterization**: Quantify radiative, microclimatic, and biological effects at plant/row scale.
3. **Operational compatibility**: Validate reversible installation and agricultural operation compatibility.
4. **Reproducibility assessment**: Document deployment constraints for similar environments.
5. **Technical de-risking**: Generate baseline data for future scale considerations.

**Timeline**: Q3 2026 instrumentation and deployment. Q4 2026 first results.  
**Scale**: 2-4 rows (100-200m linear deployment).

***

## Why Partner Now

Three converging trends make this pilot strategically timely:

1. **Land constraint**: Abandoned mountain vineyard areas represent underutilized renewable potential without agricultural land competition.
2. **Firm power demand**: Critical digital workloads require local, dispatchable energy solutions beyond intermittent generation.
3. **Regulatory momentum**: Territorial energy sovereignty and land rehabilitation are gaining priority across Europe.

This pilot positions partners at the intersection of these trends with a concrete, measurable deliverable.
