# Executive Thesis

FractaVolta proposes a simple but radical hypothesis:

**A sufficiently replicated network of autonomous territorial nodes can become more resilient than centralized infrastructure, while generating local prosperity, democratic capacity, and strategic sovereignty.**

Its elementary unit is not the state, nor the corporation, nor the data center.

Its elementary unit is:

**the autonomous commune as infrastructure node.**

A commune may become simultaneously:

* productive node
* energy node
* compute node
* democratic node

and federate with others.

This is the core proposition.

---

# 1. Problem

Contemporary infrastructures concentrate:

* energy production
* critical computation
* financial rents
* decision power

Concentration generates efficiency.

But also:

* fragility
* capture
* dependency
* opacity

FractaVolta begins from another premise:

**Resilience may require distributed redundancy rather than optimal concentration.**
